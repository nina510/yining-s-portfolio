EchoVille: Listening Training Adventure

Due to long-term hearing loss, those hearing-impaired’s ability to process sounds diminishes over time. Children with hearing impairments are found to face considerable communication challenges in about 10%-20% of situations, even with the assistance of hearing aids. This project aims to design an intelligent product that incorporates training game in simulated scenarios to help hearing-impaired children practice their sound differentiation and pronunciation skills, enabling them to communicate more effectively with others.

Background: Why do hearing-impaired children still have communication difficulties even with hearing aids?

Feature

Decreased speech discrimination ability

It may reduce the activity of the auditory nerve and affect the ability of processing audio signals.

Lack of sound stimulation

It is the basis of language learning. Through audio stimulation, individuals are able to recognize different tones, syllables and speech features.

Difficulty communicating

Individuals may have difficulty understanding what others say, especially in complex or rapid conversations.
